FAQS

Who can donate blood?

Most healthy individuals aged 18 to 65, who weigh at least 50 kg (110 lbs), and meet certain health criteria are eligible to donate blood.

Is it safe to donate blood?

Yes, donating blood is generally safe. We follow strict procedures to ensure the safety of both donors and recipients.

How often can I donate blood?

For whole blood donations, you can typically donate every 8 weeks (56 days).

What should I do before donating blood?

To prepare for a blood donation, ensure you are well-hydrated, have eaten a light meal, and had enough rest.

Does donating blood hurt?

The pain associated with blood donation is usually minimal. You may feel a slight pinch when the needle is inserted, but discomfort is generally brief and manageable.

How long does the blood donation process take?

The entire donation process, including registration, medical screening, and actual donation, usually takes about 30 to 60 minutes.

Can I donate if I have recently traveled or received a vaccine?

Travel or recent vaccination may affect your eligibility temporarily. Depending on the destination and vaccine received, there may be deferral periods.

What happens to my donated blood?

After donation, your blood goes through a series of tests to ensure it is safe for transfusion. Once cleared, it is stored and ready to be used for patients in need.

Can I donate blood if I have a cold or flu?

If you have an active infection, including a cold or flu, you should wait until you have fully recovered before donating blood.

Can I donate blood during my menstrual period?

Yes, you can donate blood during your menstrual period if you feel healthy and meet all other eligibility criteria.
